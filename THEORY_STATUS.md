# THEORY_STATUS.md

## Theory Status

Continuity Theory (also referred to as Integrity Persistence Theory) is an active research framework.

The purpose of this repository is not to present a finalized scientific theory. It is to develop a shared vocabulary for examining persistence, identity, inheritance, stewardship, risk, failure, adaptation, and collapse across scales.

The framework emerged from attempts to identify common structural patterns appearing in:

* biological evolution
* information systems
* literature
* institutions
* civilizations
* technologies
* artificial intelligence
* personal identity

The central hypothesis is that many apparently distinct phenomena can be understood as expressions of continuity dynamics.

This repository should be treated as:

* an evolving conceptual framework
* a research notebook
* a taxonomy under active revision
* a collection of analytical tools

rather than a completed academic work.

### Current Status

The framework presently contains:

* Core continuity definitions
* Integrity concepts
* Stewardship concepts
* Debt and drift concepts
* Continuity ecology concepts
* Failure mode taxonomies
* Risk analysis structures

Several areas remain under active investigation, including:

* consciousness
* continuity metrics
* continuity economics
* continuity ecology
* recursive systems
* artificial intelligence
* mathematical formalization

### Intellectual Position

The framework does not claim that continuity explains everything.

In fact, one of its identified failure modes is Framework Closure:

A continuity becoming unable to accept corrective inheritance from reality.

For that reason the framework itself is treated as a continuity subject to revision, correction, fragmentation, replacement, or termination.

The repository exists to facilitate that process.
