# continuitytheorykernal
A continuity-theory kernel for analyzing identity, persistence, risk, stewardship, inheritance, failure modes, and complex systems across stories, institutions, technologies, ecosystems, and civilizations.
This project is an active research framework. Concepts, terminology, and taxonomies are expected to evolve as the theory develops.

# Continuity Kernel

Continuity Kernel is an experimental framework for analyzing systems through the lens of persistence.

Rather than treating entities as static objects, Continuity Theory treats them as continuities: organizational structures that persist through transformation via constitutive inheritance.

The framework provides concepts, taxonomies, prompts, and machine-readable schemas for exploring:

* Continuity and identity
* Integrity and correspondence
* Stewardship and transmission
* Debt, drift, and overload
* Failure modes and collapse
* Recursive systems and self-reference
* Continuity ecology across scales

The framework can be applied to:

* Literature and narrative analysis
* Artificial intelligence
* Institutions and organizations
* Scientific theories
* Civilizations and history
* Risk assessment
* Ecology and evolution
* Consciousness and identity

A central premise of the framework is that identity emerges from continuity rather than continuity emerging from identity.

The project is intended as an open, evolving research artifact and conceptual toolkit rather than a finalized theory.
