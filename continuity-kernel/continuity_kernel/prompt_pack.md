# Continuity Kernel Prompt Pack

## General analysis prompt

Apply the Continuity / Integrity Persistence lens to the following subject:

`{subject}`

Analyze it as a continuity rather than merely an object.

Address:

1. What continuity is being examined?
2. What persists through transformation?
3. What is actually inherited?
4. What is represented as inherited?
5. What lineage is traceable?
6. Where is integrity strong?
7. Where is correspondence weak?
8. Where is debt accumulating?
9. What stewardship mechanisms preserve transmission?
10. Where can corrective inheritance enter?
11. Is this success or failure scale-dependent?
12. Is the subject adapting, drifting, fragmenting, restoring, terminating, or becoming a simulacrum?

End with one compressed thesis sentence.

## Risk assessment prompt

Apply catastrophic risk assessment through the Continuity lens.

Subject: `{subject}`

Do not replace conventional risk analysis. Add a continuity layer.

Identify:

- Subject continuity
- Parent continuities
- Child continuities
- Supporting continuities
- Inheritance pathways
- Correction pathways
- Single points of stewardship failure
- Traceability failures
- Debt mispricing
- Overload
- Drift
- Recursive closure
- Ecological cascade potential
- Adaptive failures versus pathological failures

Final question:

Where can corrective inheritance no longer enter the system?

## Literary analysis prompt

Analyze `{work}` through the Continuity lens.

Focus on:

- What continuity is under pressure?
- What lineage is being preserved, denied, restored, or corrupted?
- Which characters function as stewards, disruptors, parasites, or correction nodes?
- Is the story about persistence, fragmentation, restoration, drift, overload, false restoration, or termination?
- How does the form of the work embody the continuity condition?
