# Anti-Patterns and Misuse Cases

This document describes common ways Continuity Lens can be misapplied.

The goal is not merely to preserve the framework, but to preserve its corrective capacity.

## Persistence Does Not Equal Goodness

A continuity may persist while losing integrity.

Examples:

* Corruption
* Organized crime
* Maladaptive institutions
* Harmful ideologies

Persistence alone is not evidence of health.

Integrity must also be evaluated.

---

## Continuity Does Not Explain Everything

Continuity Lens is a perspective, not a universal replacement for all other forms of analysis.

The framework should complement:

* science
* engineering
* economics
* history
* psychology
* ecology

rather than attempting to replace them.

---

## Drift Is Not Automatically Failure

Change is inevitable.

The relevant question is not:

"Has the continuity changed?"

but:

"Does the continuity remain traceable and correspond to reality?"

---

## Fragmentation Is Not Automatically Failure

Fragmentation can produce:

* adaptation
* diversification
* specialization
* speciation

Some continuities survive precisely because they fragment.

---

## Restoration Is Not Automatically Desirable

Attempts to restore a continuity may fail when the restoration target is fictional rather than historical.

False restoration is itself a recognized failure mode.

---

## Continuity Is Not Morality

A continuity may be healthy while pursuing harmful goals.

A continuity may be ethical while struggling to persist.

The framework evaluates persistence structures, not moral worth.

---

## Beware Framework Closure

Every framework risks becoming immune to correction.

If observations repeatedly conflict with the framework, the framework should be revised.

The framework itself is a continuity and remains subject to:

* correction
* adaptation
* fragmentation
* replacement

Recursive closure is considered a failure mode of the framework itself.

---

## The Test

A useful application of Continuity Lens should reveal:

* hidden dependencies
* stewardship pathways
* debt accumulation
* correction mechanisms
* ecological relationships

If it merely re-labels existing observations, it is not adding value.
